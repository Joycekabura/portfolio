Monitoring Chalbi Desert

This project investigated land cover change and desertification trends in the Chalbi Desert, northern Kenya, as part of a broader environmental research study. The objective was to assess vegetation cover, soil texture and moisture presence in order to understand environmental conditions prior to fieldwork and to establish a baseline for long-term desertification monitoring.

To assess long-term trends, I generated environmental index and land cover maps for four time periods between 1990 and 2020. Visual inspection of these outputs indicated a clear expansion of arid and sparsely vegeted areas over time, suggesting progressive desertification across parts of Chalbi region.

Methodology

  1. Collected multi-temporal Landsat imagery for selected years between 1990 and 2020
  2. Computed MSAVI, Soil texture and Soil Moisture indices
  3. Generated environmental index maps for each time period
  4. Performed visual and qualitative change detection to assess desert expansion

Tools Used

  • Google Earth Engine
  • Python
  • Landsat 5, 7 and 8
